Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for Small Industries
(Set up by Govt. of India and SIDBI)            

Press Release on August 08, 2003

The Board of Trustees of Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for Small Industries (CGTSI) at its meeting held on August 7, 2003, has decided to remove the minimum credit limit under the scheme. CGTSI would now cover all loans up to Rs.25 lakh extended to SSI units by its Member Lending Institutions (MLIs) comprising Scheduled Commercial Banks and select Regional Rural Banks (RRBs), National Small Industries Corporation Ltd., and North Eastern Development Finance Corporation Ltd.

The Board of Trustees of CGTSI has also widened the scope of the credit guarantee scheme and would now extend guarantee cover to all loans given to SSI units including Small Scale Service & Business (Industry Related) Enterprises (SSSBE).

In all 35 eligible lending institutions have registered with CGTSI as its MLIs.  As on July 31, 2003, CGTSI has approved guarantee cover to 22 MLIs, which have extended collateral free credit to 8600 SSI units.  With the widening of the scope of guarantee scheme and making eligible all loans even below Rs.5 lakh to SSI, it is expected that banking system will step up the flow of collateral free credit to the SSI sector under credit guarantee scheme.
