Mission for the Millennium of Ministry of SSI & ARI is to create a sound policy environment to help the SSI sector to cope with the emerging challenges.  One of the measures is to strengthen credit delivery system through credit guarantee scheme which will help flow of collateral-free credit flow to the sector. 

Hon’ble Prime Minister formally launched the Credit Guarantee Scheme implemented through CGTSI on August 30, 2000 at Vigyan Bhavan, New Delhi when it was also announced to enhance the eligible loan limit under the Scheme from Rs.10 lakh to Rs.25 lakh. 

Board of Trustees of CGTSI at its meeting held on September 26, 2000 approved modifications to the Scheme giving effect to the enhancement in the eligible credit limit under the Scheme to Rs.25 lakh as also certain suggestions received from Indian Banks’ Association (IBA) to bring about operational flexibility. 

Eligible lending institutions are Scheduled Commercial Banks, Regional Rural Banks (which have been classified by NABARD under the category - ‘Sustainable Viability’) and National Small Industries Corporation Ltd.(NSIC).  To avail of guarantee facility, these eligible lending institutions are required to become Member-Lending Institutions of CGTSI by entering into an agreement. 

CGTSI has been creating greater awareness of the guarantee scheme through seminars, writing letters to all the eligible institutions as also to the small  industry associations.  CGTSI also participates in the State Level Bankers’ Committee meetings. I am happy to inform that IBA has constituted a Review Forum, comprising senior officials from four banks viz. Punjab National Bank, Central Bank of India, Vijaya Bank and United Bank of India.   Shri K. C. Chowdhary, Chief Executive & Secretary, IBA will represent IBA in the Review Forum.   Exchange of views between IBA Review Forum and CGTSI will help Member-Lending Institutions and the Trust to build mutually beneficial working relationship in operationalising the guarantee scheme.

Scheduled commercial banks which have become Member-Lending Institutions of CGTSI are Punjab National Bank, Punjab & Sind Bank, Bank of Baroda, Dena Bank, Union Bank of India, Indian Overseas Bank and HDFC Bank.  Boards of Directors of Andhra Bank, Bank of India, Central Bank of India, Corporation Bank and Vijaya Bank have approved these banks becoming Member-Lending Institutions of CGTSI and these banks are expected to complete the formalities shortly.  Other scheduled commercial banks have indicated their willingness to participate in the guarantee scheme. National Small Industries Corporation Ltd. is also a Member-Lending Institution of CGTSI.

The Guarantee Scheme launched by GOI will help SSI at large, particularly, the millions of first generation entrepreneurs, in the field of Information Technology and Software, to realise their dream of setting up productive enterprises and contribute to the economic development of the country.  I am sure the eligible lending institutions would perceive the credit guarantee as a useful alternative instrument in place of collateral security, when availing of guarantee cover mitigates 75% of their risk.

Under the Guarantee Scheme, all the eligible proposals have to be lodged to the Trust within 90 days from the date of sanction for obtaining guarantee cover from CGTSI. In order to help those Member-Lending Institutions who joined the scheme recently, Board of Trustees at its meeting held on December 9, 2000 approved the Trust covering all eligible proposals of the Member-Lending Institutions sanctioned on or after June 1, 2000 provided the proposals are submitted to CGTSI by January 31, 2001.

At a function held at Indian Overseas Bank, Chennai on January 3, 2001, Shri Devi Dayal, Special Secretary (Banking), Ministry of Finance, GOI handed over sanction letters issued by 7 banks to the borrowers whose loans are being covered under Credit Guarantee Scheme.
