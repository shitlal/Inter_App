Dear Viewer,

The Credit Guarantee Fund Trust for Micro and Small Enterprises (CGTMSE) was set up by Ministry of Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises (MSME), Government of Inda (GOI) and Small Industries Bank of India (SIDBI) in August 2000. The GOI and SIDBI as settlors of the Trust have committed to raise a corpus of Rs.25 billion (approx.USD 625 million ) in the ratio of 4 : 1 to provide CGTMSE a sound financial base. CGTMSE operates the “Credit Guarantee Scheme” (CGS) which facilitates provision of collateral free and third party guarantee free credit facilities to units in the micro and small enterprises sector.

There have been widespread complaints that owing to inability to arrange collateral security and / or third party guarantee,many entrepreneurs particularly technocrats and first generation entrepreneurs in the micro and small enterprises sector find it extremely difficult to access credit from the banking system. The banks on their part are concerned by the rate of defaults in the small loans segment and hence try to collateralize their exposure to this sector. CGS, therefore, plays a significant role in providing the much needed comfort to banks and other Member Lending Institutions (MLIs) for extending credit facilities to this sector. The guarantee mechanism provides a new window of hope to the entrepreneurs in the micro and small enterprises sector across the country in achieving their objective of setting up their own enterprises or for undertaking expansion, diversification or modification of their existing enterprises without support of collateral security. It also facilitates the MLIs to evaluate the credit proposals on the basis of intrinsic merits of the project rather than merely on availability of adequate collaterals.

Under the CGS, credit facilities including term loans, fund and non-fund based working capital facilities up to Rs.5 million (approx.USD 125000) extended by MLIs to micro and small enterprises (both in the manufacturing and service sectors excluding retail trade ) are guaranteed upto 75% of the amount in default, subject to a maximum of Rs.3.75 million (approx. USD 93750). In respect of loans to micro sector up to Rs.0.50 million (approx. USD 12500), micro and small enterprises operated/owned by women and units located in North Eastern Region (NER) including Sikkim, the coverage is 80%. One time guarantee fee of 1.5% of the credit facility sanctioned (0.75% for NER including Sikkim) and Annual Service Fee of 0.75% are collected from the MLIs. Loans guaranteed under the scheme carry zero per cent risk weight/provision for the MLI for the guaranteed portion.

CGTMSE provides free Life Insurance cover of Rs.0.20 million (approx. USD 5000) to Chief Promoters of the enterprises which are covered under CGS and where the guarantees are valid.

Partner institutions availing CGS are Scheduled Commercial Banks (Public, Private and Foreign banks), Regional Rural Banks (classified as sustainably viable by NABARD), National Small Industries Corporation Ltd., North Eastern Development Finance Corporation Ltd. and Small Industries Development Bank of India. A link to the list of MLIs is provided in the home page.

CGTMSE provides on line business transaction facility to the MLIs and has a robust and user friendly IT system in place.

MLIs are requested to cover as many proposals as possible under CGS. The request for guarantee cover should be made as soon as the credit facilities are sanctioned to the eligible borrowers. In any case the application for cover in respect of sanctions effected in one calendar quarter should be submitted by the MLI latest by the end of the following calendar quarter.

Circulars issued by CGTMSE from time to time on CGS related issues are available on the website for ready reference.
